## Meaning

Avinash Vyas's great philosophical bhajan about the human condition seen from above. The refrain: *"Toys of ash — my Ram has kept us playing; he took the earth of the mortal world and shaped it into human forms."* The toys speak and sway and play new games every day, declaring "this is mine, this is yours" and quarrelling with each other over nothing. From the unbaked clay of the body, color is painted on — maya — and the dolls set up houses, and then, in no time, the fan is already being waved over the bier. The thread between finite and infinite never broke, and the game stays unfinished — the body's and mind's unsaid things remain unsaid forever. At a besnu, it stops grief mid-sentence: we are all toys of ash; one has simply gone back into the potter's hand first.
