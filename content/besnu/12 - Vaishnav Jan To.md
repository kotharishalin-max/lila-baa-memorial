## Meaning

Narsinh Mehta's defining Gujarati bhajan — famously Mahatma Gandhi's favorite — sets out, in four short stanzas, the ideal of a *Vaishnav jan* (a devotee of Vishnu). Such a person is one who feels another's pain as their own; who does good for the suffering without pride; who bows to all and slanders none; who keeps tongue, deed, and mind steady (and whose mother is therefore blessed); whom worldly illusion cannot touch because their renunciation is firm; in whose mind Ram's name is the only chime, and in whose body all pilgrimage sites dwell; who is free of greed and deceit, and has overcome lust and anger. Narsinh concludes: *"to merely behold such a one is to liberate seventy-one generations of one's family."* At Lila Baa's besnu, the bhajan doubles as a family portrait — the life we are called to live in her memory.
