## Meaning

A beloved Gujarati / Jain devotional song about the gulf between divine abundance and human receptivity. The refrain — "You rained down with an open heart; we have been thirsty for lifetimes" — frames every verse. Though the divine gives with a thousand hands, our bowl stays empty. Though a treasury of wisdom is poured out, we remain ignorant. A Ganga of affection flows to purify our lives; a flame of love is lit to brighten our souls; the sun rises to shine — yet we keep wandering in darkness and stop at the shore of the ocean that has come to meet us. At a besnu, it reframes grief: the departed is the one who arrived; the living are the ones still learning to receive.
