## Meaning

A bhajan of absolute, unconditional surrender to Shrinathji — the central gesture of the Pushtimarg tradition. The opening couplet is the devotee's one reserved request before everything else is let go: *"Keep everything yours, but hear my one plea — give this child a place at the Sadguru's holy feet."* Then the refrain — *"I have surrendered everything to you, Lord — Jay Shrinathji"* — becomes the answer to every binary the verses raise: save me or drown me, keep me alive or let me die; lift me up or drop me, reject me or accept me; grant me your seva or hold me at a distance; give me your remembrance or let me wander in maya; give me satsang or keep me in bad company; give me darshan or leave me roaming. Each outcome is equal once the surrender is complete — the theology the Pushtimarg tradition calls *Brahma Sambandha* (total self-dedication to Krishna). At Lila Baa's besnu it carries two audiences at once: it describes how she lived, having entrusted herself to Shrinathji, and it is the family now placing her into His keeping, asking only that she be received at the Sadguru's feet.
