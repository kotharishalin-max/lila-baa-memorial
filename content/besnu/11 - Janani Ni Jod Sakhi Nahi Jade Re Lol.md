## Meaning

Damodar Khushaldas Botadkar's most-loved Gujarati poem. Its refrain — *"Sakhi, no one matches a mother"* — echoes after every couplet. Each verse is a comparison: honey is sweet and monsoon rain is sweet, but my mother is sweeter; she is God's own puppet of love, a class apart from the world; her eyes are filled with nectar, her words with affection; her hands woven of silk, her heart the stirring of winter; the gods themselves could not milk such milk from her; her finger is the support of the world, her heart is brimming with desires; her mind is on the potter's wheel, every instant tied to your breath; her silent blessings swell in her chest and never run out; even the earth might tremble, but a mother is unfailingly firm; the Ganga's waters rise and fall, but her love's current is level; the sky-cloud rains only sometimes, but a mother's cloud pours all twelve months; even a moving moon's moonlight seems to set, but a mother's light never sets. Sung at Lila Baa's besnu — a ceremony for a mother and grandmother — it becomes its own eulogy.
