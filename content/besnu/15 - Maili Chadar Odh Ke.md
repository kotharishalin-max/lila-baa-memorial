## Meaning

A traditional Hindi bhajan of self-accounting. The singer stands at the divine threshold in a *maili chadar* — a soiled shawl — and asks, *"How can I come to your door wearing this? O holy Parameshwar, I am ashamed in my own mind."* Each verse is a confession: you sent me into the world with a spotless body, and I stained it; I received a pure voice, and never sang your name; I shut my eyes and never meditated on you; the strings of my mind's veena are broken, so what song could I sing you now? My feet never carried me to your temple; my head never bowed at your altars; *"O Harihar, I have come to you defeated — what garland can I now offer?"* At a besnu the bhajan's angle is particular: it is not about the departed but about those left behind, recognizing what of devotion went unlived in our own time, and asking to be received at the door nonetheless.
