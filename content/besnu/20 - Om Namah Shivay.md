## Meaning

A prayer-lullaby built on Shiva's five-syllable mantra *Om Namah Shivaya* ("I bow to Shiva") — the mantra repeats on the breath-scale (*saanson ki sargam*), and every heartbeat echoes it. The two verses are a shared human confession at any hour of loss: *"What darkness has fallen over life; doubt has surrounded us; the mind-bird, God, is deeply afraid today."* Then: *"The rosary of faith has broken and lies scattered; Lord, grant us support — this is a difficult hour; showing us the way, we have come to your refuge."* At the besnu, the bhajan leans on the mantra's resolution: however broken the faith, the breath itself is already doing the prayer.
