## Meaning

A Gujarati prayer built around a single refrain — *"Your mercy has no limit"* — repeated after each verse of confession. The devotee names every failure: sins so heavy that service has been forgotten; poison cups drunk willingly; moves played crookedly; a shore not found, a destination unclear. After each failing, the divine is addressed with a new name: *Remover of troubles, Forgetter of our mistakes, Turner of poison into nectar, Setter-right of what we set wrong, True Boatman, Sweet-shade-giver.* The penultimate verse carries the bhajan's emotional core: *"Even if the child turns unworthy, you are still called the parent."* At a besnu, it is a prayer for the departed — asking the merciful Lord to receive Lila Baa at his feet — and a plea for the mourners: that mercy without limit extends to those left behind as well.
