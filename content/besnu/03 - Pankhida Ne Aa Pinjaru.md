## Meaning

A philosophical Gujarati bhajan by Avinash Vyas using the old-bird-tiring-of-its-cage metaphor: the soul (pankhida) has grown restless in the body (pinjaru). No matter how much we reason with the bird, it wants a new cage. An unsettled longing has risen in its very breath; unbidden, it has set its heart on a distant departure to an unseen land. You may offer it a gold-trimmed perch, a gold-trimmed swing, a diamond-studded fan, priceless pearl ornaments — none of it holds; once the dye of another place has set in, the bird has already chosen. At a besnu, the song says what the ceremony is for: to let the bird go, and to understand that its going was its own wish, not an accident.
