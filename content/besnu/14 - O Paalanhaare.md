## Meaning

A. R. Rahman and Javed Akhtar's devotional song from *Lagaan* — voiced in the film by villagers praying for rain — has outlived the movie to become one of the most-sung memorial prayers in India. The address is to the *Paalanhaare*, the formless sustainer, *nirgun and apart*, without whom we have no one. It is the moon that holds moonlight because of you; the sun that glows because of you; the happy sky that has stars because you placed them; if you do not shape our life, who will? A second verse is an appeal: grant patience to the suffering so they are never defeated by sorrow; protect the weak so they may live in peace; give strength to devotion; listen, master of the world, this one small request — there is darkness on our path, bless us with light. At Lila Baa's besnu, it holds both sides of the gathering: those asking for strength in grief, and those trusting that the formless Paalanhaare has received her.
