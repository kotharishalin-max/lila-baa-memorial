## Meaning

A classical Gujarati philosophical bhajan shaped entirely around one metaphor: life is a cart, and Hari holds the reins. The singer says, *"Hari, where are you taking my cart? I have no idea."* The bullocks yoked to it are *dharma* and *karma*; the rein is *patience.* The cart rolls on wheels of joy and sorrow; sometimes the sun of hope rises, sometimes darkness falls; the driver can't tell where their destination lies. Dreams are stored in the trunk of the eyelids and locked behind the chain of the mind; town after town comes along the road, but never the pilgrim's Kashi; who knows when this hostile night will end and morning's breeze will blow? The last verse is the bhajan's climax: *"From where did I come, where am I going, where must I stay? This play of agam and nigam is unknowable; the mind keeps getting muddled; the body wandering around is just an old cage."* At a besnu, the bhajan reframes the grief: if Hari held the reins, then the journey has ended exactly where it was meant to.
