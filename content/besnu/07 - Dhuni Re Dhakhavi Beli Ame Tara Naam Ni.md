## Meaning

A classic Gujarati Nirguni (attributeless-divine) bhajan. The refrain — *"We have lit the sacred fire in your name, O friend"* — frames a singer-pilgrim who has taken up the name of a formless Lord (Alakh) as the only flame worth tending. The verses describe a lost swan/soul (hanso) that flew into the courtyard and got battered along the roads, unable to find the way to the Thakur's abode; a yearning for something ineffable; and a thirst for the dwelling of the Alakh that nothing else can slake. At a besnu, it fits the besnu's own gesture: we sit around this fire and repeat the name of the One who has no form, trusting the departed soul will find its way home.
