# Tu Pyar Ka Sagar Hai — You Are the Ocean of Love

Part of [[Lila Baa Besnu]] — track 21 of 25.

Shailendra's lyrics and Shankar-Jaikishan's music for Manna Dey, from the 1955 film *Seema* — a filmi bhajan that has long since become a universal prayer-meeting standard.

## Meaning

Shailendra's lyrics for the 1955 film *Seema* (a name that means "boundary" or "limit") — sung famously by Manna Dey — have long detached from their film and settled into prayer meetings. The opening is the bhajan's argument in one line: *"You are the ocean of love; we are thirsty for a single drop of you; and when you take back what you have given, we will depart from this world."* The first verse describes the wounded mind as a restless bird — its wings soft, eyes blurry — that has to cross an ocean, and it asks the divine to explain the way, since it has forgotten where it came from. The second verse holds the exact mood of the besnu: *"On one side life sings swaying, on the other side death stands; no one knows where the boundary lies, and a tangle has fallen upon us — whisper in our ear from which direction we came."* The question — *where did we come from?* — is the one that makes a besnu meaningful.
