# He Nath Jodi Hath — Paramatma, Grant the Soul True Peace

Part of [[Lila Baa Besnu]] — track 25 of 25 (the closing prayer).

## Romanized (English script)

He Nath! Jodi hath paaye prem-thi sau laagiye,
Sharaṇu male saachu tamaaru… e hriday-thi maangiye;
Je jeev aavyo aap paase… charan-ma apnaavjo,
Paramatma e aatma-ne… shaanti saachi aapjo.

Vali karma-na yoge kari… je kul-ma e avtare,
Tya purṇa preme o Prabhu-ji! Aap-ni bhakti kare,
Laksh choryaashi bandhan-o-ne… laksh-ma lai-ne kaapjo,
Paramatma…

Sukh sampatti suvichaaro ne satkarm-no dai varso,
Janmo-janam satsang-thi Kirtaar paar utaarjo.
Aa lok-ne par-lok-ma tuj prem rag-rag vyaapjo,
Paramatma…

Male moksh ke sukh swarga-na aasha ure evi nathi,
Malyo deh durlabh maanvi-no seva karva bhaav-thi,
Saachu bataavi roop Shri Arihant hriday-e sthaapjo,
Paramatma…

## Meaning

The traditional Gujarati *Shradhanjali Stavan* — the final-tribute prayer sung at the close of a besnu. Each verse ends with the refrain that gives the song its alternate title: *"Paramatma, grant the soul true peace."* The first verse, which names the gesture of the ceremony itself: *"O Lord, with folded hands and feet we cling to you in love; we beg from the heart for your true refuge; this soul that has come to you — adopt them into your feet; Paramatma, grant the soul true peace."* The second asks that whichever family, through the play of karma, the soul is reborn into, let them give full-hearted devotion there, and let the eighty-four lakh cycles of bondage be cut. The third asks for a rain of happiness, wealth, right thought, and right action, and that the Lord carry this soul across through the company of the devoted, birth after birth. The closing verse is the bhajan's clearest voice: *"I carry no hope of moksha or the happiness of heaven; I have received this rare human body so that I may serve with feeling; show me the true form of Arihant and install it in my heart."* At the end of Lila Baa's besnu, it is the ceremony's own benediction — asked out loud, one final time, for her.
