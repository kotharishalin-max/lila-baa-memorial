## Meaning

Sant Puneet Maharaj's defining Gujarati bhajan, and the song Shalin chose to sing for his grandmother at her besnu. Its first couplet is the whole thesis: *"Forget anything else in the world, but do not forget your mother and father; their benefactions are beyond counting, never let them slip from memory."* The verses name the debt in images: you worshipped stones on the earth before you ever saw their face, so never harden your own heart into stone and crush theirs; they pulled morsels from their own mouths to feed yours and made you grown, so never spit poison at those who gave you nectar; they indulged a thousand desires of yours and fulfilled every wish, so do not now forget the wishes of those who fulfilled yours; if your millions cannot make your parents warm, then your millions are not riches but ash; if you want your own children to serve you, serve yours — you reap what you sow; they slept on the wet side of the bed so you could sleep on the dry side, so do not now make those nectar-filled eyes weep; they strewed flowers on your road, so do not turn into a thorn on theirs; with money you can buy back everything, but you cannot buy back parents — *never forget the yearning to kiss those sacred feet, moment by moment*. Sung by Shalin for Lila Baa, it is both elegy and instruction — what he is saying to her, and what she taught everyone who grew up in her house.
