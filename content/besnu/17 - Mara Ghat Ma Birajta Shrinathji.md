## Meaning

A core bhajan of the Pushtimarg Vaishnav tradition, the sect with which the Kothari family's memorial vocabulary ("Shriji charan pamya che" — *reached Shriji's feet*) is aligned. The refrain *"Shrinathji, Yamunaji, Mahaprabhuji dwell within my heart"* names the three gurus of the sect: Shrinathji (Krishna at Nathdwara), Yamunaji (the river-goddess), and Mahaprabhuji (Shri Vallabhacharya, the founder). Each verse builds an inner geography: my mind is Gokul and Vrindavan; my body's courtyard holds forests of tulsi; my eyes see Girdhari; my heart is sold to Shyam Morari; I have taken darshan of Vallabh Prabhu; I do Vitthal's daily service and the eightfold jhaanki (the day's eight ritual viewings). *"I set the path of bhakti — and then the path of pushti (grace) — as my road; kirtan caught me; I asked for the ruby of the Lala's glow, and the diamond came to my hand."* The last verse is the besnu's exact wish: *"Hear my last-hour plea — Shrijibawa, take me in your refuge; let no messenger of Yama ever come, let my own Lord call me home."* Sung at Lila Baa's besnu, it is the prayer the family is making for her — and the path she walked herself.
